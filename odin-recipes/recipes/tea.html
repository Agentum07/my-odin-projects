Masala Tea

Description

Indulge in the warm and aromatic embrace of Masala Tea, a cherished beverage from India. This spiced tea is a delightful fusion of robust black tea and a fragrant blend of spices, creating a rich and invigorating experience for your senses.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 2 tablespoons of loose black tea leaves or 4 tea bags
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, grated
  • 1-2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4-5 black peppercorns
  • 2-3 tablespoons of sugar (adjust to taste)
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1-2 teaspoons of tea masala spice blend (available in stores) or a mix of ground cloves, cinnamon, and black pepper

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, bring the water and milk to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  2. Add the tea leaves or tea bags, crushed cardamom pods, cloves, grated ginger, cinnamon sticks, and black peppercorns to the simmering liquid.
  3. Stir in the sugar and saffron strands, if desired. Adjust the sugar to your preferred sweetness.
  4. Allow the tea to simmer for 10-15 minutes, ensuring the flavors infuse well. The longer you simmer, the stronger the spice flavor will be.
  5. Add the tea masala spice blend or a mix of ground cloves, cinnamon, and black pepper. Stir well and let it simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  6. Remove the saucepan from heat and strain the masala tea into cups or mugs. Discard the tea leaves and spices.
  7. Serve your hot and aromatic Masala Tea with your choice of snacks, biscuits, or enjoy it on its own for a soothing and invigorating experience.

Enjoy your tea!
